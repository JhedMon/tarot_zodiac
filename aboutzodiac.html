What is Zodiac?

The word Zodiac is used in astronomy and astrology to refer to the band of sky over which the sun, moon, and planets move as seen from Earth, at least. The zodiac contains a number of constellations, and the sun (or other celestial body) is said to be “in” a constellation when it moves into the portion of the zodiac that includes that constellation. The zodiac can be traced back to ancient civilizations, such as the ancient Greeks and Romans, who believed the position of heavenly bodies could be used to predict the future. For them, astronomy and astrology were one and the same which is definitely not the case anymore!

In astrology, a sign of the zodiac refers to one of the 12 special signs of the zodiac through which the sun passes. A person's special horoscope is the sign in which the sun was at the time of his birth. In astrology, it is believed that a person's personality can be predicted by their zodiac sign. According to astrology, it is also possible to predict the future by looking at the position of the sun, moon and planets compared to the constellations of the zodiac. An astrological chart of planets and zodiac signs is called a horoscope; the word horoscope is also used to predict the future using such a chart
