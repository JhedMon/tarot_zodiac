What is Tarot?

Tarot cards are a deck of cards used for divination, spiritual guidance, and personal reflection. Originating in the 15th century as a card game, tarot evolved into a tool for esoteric purposes by the late 18th century. A standard tarot deck consists of 78 cards divided into two main parts: the Major Arcana, comprising 22 cards that depict significant life events and spiritual lessons, and the Minor Arcana , consisting of 56 cards that address everyday situations and personal interactions. Each card has its own symbolism and meanings, which are interpreted by readers to provide insights and guidance.

The Rider-Waite Tarot, also known as the Rider-Waite-Smith Tarot, is one of the most popular and widely used tarot decks. Created in 1909 by artist Pamela Colman Smith under the direction of mystic and scholar Arthur Edward Waite, it was published by the Rider Company. This deck is renowned for its detailed, symbolic imagery, which has set a standard for many subsequent tarot decks. The Major Arcana cards in the Rider-Waite Tarot depict archetypal figures and themes, while the Minor Arcana cards are richly illustrated with scenes that convey their meanings more clearly than earlier, simpler designs. The Rider-Waite Tarot remains a foundational tool for both beginners and experienced tarot readers.
